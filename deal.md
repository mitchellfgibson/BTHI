  <span class="deal-section-label">The thesis</span>
  <h2>Why I backed this</h2>
  <p>Meridian's core technology is an acoustic-signature recognition model trained on ONR-provided datasets that have taken the Navy fifteen years and substantial classified resources to compile. The founders — Dr. James Okafor and Dr. Lisa Huang — ran the DARPA Ocean Systems program between 2018 and 2022. They didn't just understand the technology. They wrote the procurement specifications that define what DoD will buy.</p>
  <p>The Phase II SBIR validates that ONR believes in the approach. The $750K pilot contract with a Tier 1 defense contractor validates that the technology works in an operational environment. The $40M valuation cap is aggressive for pre-revenue, and I want to be honest about that — I used a comp set anchored to Vannevar Labs and Epirus at similar stages, and I believe $40M is defensible but not conservative. Members should treat this as a high-risk, long-horizon position.</p>
  <p>My investment thesis in one sentence: the founders have non-replicable access to classified training data and procurement relationships. That moat is real and it is narrow. If this works, it works because of those two things.</p>
  <div class="thesis-sig">— Sarah Chen, Partner, Paladin Capital &nbsp;·&nbsp; Investing $250,000 on these terms</div>
</div>

<div class="deal-section">
  <span class="deal-section-label">Why this deal exists here</span>
  <h2>Why Meridian is raising on Unaccredited</h2>
  <p>Meridian chose community capital deliberately. Their early customer base — contracting officers, program managers, veterans in the defense procurement community — is geographically dispersed across mid-size cities and largely non-accredited by income. The founders wanted those people as shareholders, not as an afterthought to a conventional venture round.</p>
  <p>We listed this deal because: (1) the lead is investing meaningful capital from their own fund, not a nominal check; (2) the terms are the same for every investor, with no institutional side letters; and (3) the company passed our diligence process, which means the lead investor is on record with a rationale that members can interrogate. We are also investing $75,000 of platform capital in this round. If Meridian fails, we lose alongside you.</p>

  <span class="deal-section-label">Risk disclosures</span>
  <h2>What can go wrong</h2>
  <p style="font-size:0.88rem; opacity:0.75; max-width:60ch;">These are not boilerplate. They are the specific risks we think are most likely to matter for this investment, written plainly.</p>
</div>

<div class="risk-section">
  <ol class="risk-list">
    <li class="risk-item">You can lose all of your money. Meridian is a pre-revenue startup with no guarantee of commercial success. The base rate for venture-stage companies returning capital to investors is below 30%. Assume the most likely outcome is zero.</li>
    <li class="risk-item">This investment is illiquid for 5–10 years, probably longer. There is no secondary market for Reg CF equity. You cannot sell your position. The only exit is a company acquisition or IPO, both of which are outside your control and may never happen.</li>
    <li class="risk-item">Government contract dependency. Meridian's survival depends on ONR continuing to fund the acoustic classification program. If program priorities shift, if the relevant budget line gets cut, or if Meridian's Phase III SBIR application is denied, the company will need to find alternative revenue quickly or fail.</li>
    <li class="risk-item">The valuation may be too high. At $40M cap, you're paying a premium that assumes this company executes on a specific commercialization path. If they miss milestones, subsequent rounds may come in at a lower valuation, diluting your position further even before considering standard dilution from new investors.</li>
    <li class="risk-item">Regulatory and export control risk. Defense technology companies face ITAR, EAR, and related export control requirements. A compliance failure could restrict operations, produce significant legal costs, or result in loss of government contracts. These risks are not hypothetical for companies working with classified training data.</li>
    <li class="risk-item">The lead investor is not your fiduciary. Sarah Chen's interest in Meridian's success is aligned with yours in a general sense, but her fund's portfolio construction, fee structure, and exit timing preferences may differ from yours in specific decisions. The 10% carry is the only formal alignment mechanism.</li>
  </ol>
